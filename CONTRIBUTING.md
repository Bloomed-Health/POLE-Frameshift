# Contributing to the POLE c.138del Research Framework

Thank you for your interest in contributing to this research. This is an active investigation into a novel POLE variant with implications for clinical genetics, cancer biology, and therapeutic strategy. We welcome contributions from researchers across multiple disciplines.

---

## Specific Collaboration Opportunities

### Structural Biology

**Need:** AlphaFold modeling of the 54-residue truncated peptide interaction with the POLE2 (p59) accessory subunit.

**What you'd contribute:** In silico binding predictions, molecular dynamics simulations, and structural analysis of the N-terminal region (residues 1–54) to evaluate the stoichiometric poisoning model (Model 3).

**Expertise required:** Computational structural biology, AlphaFold2/3, protein-protein interaction modeling.

---

### Bioinformatics — DepMap / Synthetic Lethality

**Need:** Systematic mining of existing POLE-mutant cell line dependency data for synthetic lethal targets.

**What you'd contribute:** Analysis of DepMap/Project Score data comparing POLE-mutant vs. POLE-wild-type cell lines, identifying differential dependencies on ATR, CHK1, WEE1, PARP1, WRN, and other druggable targets.

**Expertise required:** Cancer dependency map analysis, CRISPR screen data, R/Python bioinformatics.

---

### Clinical Genetics — POLE Patient Cohorts

**Need:** Retrospective phenotyping of patients carrying POLE truncating variants currently classified as VUS.

**What you'd contribute:** Clinical case data, family histories, and tumor genomic profiles from patients with POLE truncating variants — especially those with unexplained polyposis, early-onset CRC, or high TMB who tested negative for APC, MUTYH, and Lynch syndrome genes.

**Expertise required:** Clinical genetics, genetic counseling, hereditary cancer panel interpretation.

---

### Immunology — Immune Profiling

**Need:** CyTOF or spectral flow cytometry immune profiling of POLE-mutant tumors.

**What you'd contribute:** Characterization of T cell subsets, exhaustion markers (PD-1, TIM-3, LAG-3, TIGIT), TCR repertoire data, and neoantigen-reactive T cell detection.

**Expertise required:** Tumor immunology, flow cytometry, TCR sequencing.

---

### Computational Biology — Crypt Dynamics Modeling

**Need:** Stochastic computational model of colonic crypt stem cell dynamics under POLE haploinsufficiency.

**What you'd contribute:** Agent-based or stochastic models predicting mutation accumulation rates, LOH probability, and time-to-adenoma as a function of POLE dosage. Calibrated against published single-cell WGS data from normal crypts.

**Expertise required:** Mathematical modeling, stochastic simulation, cancer evolution modeling.

---

### Literature Mining / NLP

**Need:** Systematic identification of POLE truncating variant carriers in published literature who may have been misclassified or overlooked.

**What you'd contribute:** Text mining of PubMed, clinical trial reports, and hereditary cancer panel studies to identify unreported PPAP cases with truncating POLE variants.

**Expertise required:** Biomedical NLP, literature mining, systematic review methodology.

---

## What You Get

- Access to the complete research framework and analysis specifications
- Potential co-authorship on resulting publications
- Collaboration with a multidisciplinary research network
- Contribution to a case with potential to reclassify an entire category of genetic variants

---

## How to Contribute

### Code and Analysis

1. Fork the repository
2. Create a feature branch (`git checkout -b feature/your-analysis`)
3. Commit your changes with clear, descriptive messages
4. Push to your fork and open a pull request
5. Include a description of what your contribution adds and how it relates to the research framework

### Case Reports and Clinical Data

Use the [POLE Truncating Variant Case Report](../../issues/new?template=pole-case-report.yml) issue template to submit structured case data.

### General Discussion

Visit the [Discussions](https://github.com/Bloomed-Health/POLE-Frameshift/discussions) tab to ask questions, propose alternative mechanistic models, share relevant observations, or offer collaboration.

### Clinical Collaboration Inquiries

For inquiries involving patient data, tissue sharing, or clinical trial design, please open a discussion thread or contact the repository maintainers through the information provided on the [Bloomed Health](https://github.com/Bloomed-Health) organization page.

---

## Code of Conduct

All contributors are expected to engage respectfully and constructively. This is a research collaboration focused on scientific rigor and patient benefit. We welcome diverse perspectives and expertise.
