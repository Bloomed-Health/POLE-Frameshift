# POLE Carrier Registry Cross-Reference

## Purpose

This document systematically examines published POLE/POLD1 carrier cohorts and databases to determine whether any other truncation variant carriers have documented connective tissue disorders (hEDS), dysautonomia (POTS), congenital anomalies, or other multi-system non-neoplastic findings. A positive finding would strengthen the causal link between POLE haploinsufficiency and the patient's expanded phenotype; absence of such reports may reflect ascertainment bias rather than true absence.

---

## Key Databases and Cohorts

### 1. PolED Database (2025)

- **Citation:** [PMID: 41263451](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41263451/)
- **Content:** Curated database of functional studies of POLE/POLD1 variants reported in humans
- **Scope:** Functional characterization data (polymerase activity, exonuclease activity, mutator phenotype) for reported variants
- **Relevance to this analysis:** The PolED database catalogs *functional* data for variants, not comprehensive clinical phenotyping of carriers. It is unlikely to contain information about hEDS, POTS, or congenital anomalies, as these are not part of standard PPAP phenotyping. However, it may identify other truncating variants with clinical descriptions that could serve as comparators.

**Query targets:**
- All POLE truncating variants (frameshift, nonsense, splice-site) in the database
- Any clinical descriptions beyond cancer/polyposis for these carriers
- Variant-level functional data that might inform whether truncation carriers show haploinsufficiency signatures

### 2. Valle Lab Cohort ; 2,813 Hereditary Cancer Probands

- **Citation:** Valle L et al. (2020) Role of POLE and POLD1 in familial cancer. *Genetics in Medicine*.
- **Content:** Sequenced POLE/POLD1 in 2,813 hereditary cancer probands; identified 12 exonuclease domain missense variants, **6 loss-of-function (LoF) variants**, and 23 outside-ED predicted-deleterious missense variants
- **Relevance:** The **6 LoF variants** are the most critical comparator set for c.138del. These are truncating variants that, like c.138del, eliminate functional domains.

**Key questions:**
- What clinical phenotypes were documented for the 6 LoF variant carriers?
- Were any non-neoplastic findings noted (connective tissue, vascular anomalies, autoimmune)?
- Were any carriers evaluated for endometriosis, congenital anomalies, or dysautonomia?
- What was the cancer spectrum in LoF carriers vs. exonuclease domain missense carriers?

**Limitation:** Clinical phenotyping in the Valle cohort focused on cancer/polyposis (the enrollment criterion). Non-neoplastic findings would only be documented if specifically assessed, which is unlikely in a hereditary cancer genetics context.

### 3. Palles et al. (2022) ; PPAP Clinical Features Cohort

- **Citation:** Palles C et al. (2022) The clinical features of PPAP and recommendations for patient management. *Familial Cancer* 21:197–209.
- **Content:** Most comprehensive clinical characterization of PPAP carriers. Describes polyp counts, cancer types, age of onset, and surveillance recommendations.
- **Relevance:** Establishes the clinical reference standard for PPAP phenotype.

**Key questions:**
- Were any congenital anomalies reported in PPAP carriers?
- Were autoimmune conditions documented?
- Were connective tissue disorders or dysautonomia mentioned?
- Was endometriosis systematically assessed?

**Expected answer:** No ; the Palles cohort focused exclusively on neoplastic phenotyping (polyps, cancers, age of onset). Non-neoplastic features were not systematically evaluated. This represents a fundamental **ascertainment bias**: if nobody looks for hEDS, POTS, or congenital anomalies in POLE carriers, absence of reports does not equal absence of findings.

### 4. Bellido et al. (2016) ; 529 Kindreds

- **Citation:** Bellido F et al. (2016) POLE and POLD1 mutations in 529 kindred with familial colorectal cancer and/or polyposis. *Genetics in Medicine* 18:325–332.
- **Content:** 529 families with familial CRC/polyposis screened for POLE/POLD1 variants.
- **Relevance:** Large cohort but ascertained through cancer genetics clinics. Clinical data limited to GI and cancer phenotypes.

### 5. Viana-Errasti et al. (2025) ; Somatic vs. Germline Comparison

- **Citation:** Viana-Errasti J et al. (2025) Comparative Analysis of Somatic and Germline Polymerase Proofreading Deficiencies in Cancer. *Modern Pathology*.
- **Content:** Molecular and clinical characteristics of 31 POLE/POLD1 ED pathogenic variants.
- **Relevance:** Establishes the molecular framework (MMR status, TMB, signatures) but focuses on tumor-level characterization rather than whole-patient phenotyping.

### 6. ClinVar and LOVD

- **Scope:** Variant-level classifications with limited clinical data
- **Relevance:** May contain case descriptions for POLE truncating variants, but clinical detail is typically minimal (phenotype often listed only as "hereditary cancer predisposition" or "polyposis")

### 7. Robinson et al. (2021) ; Normal Tissue Mutation Rates

- **Citation:** Robinson PS et al. (2021) *Nature Genetics*.
- **Content:** Germline POLE/POLD1 carriers assessed for somatic mutation rates in normal tissues using NanoSeq.
- **Relevance:** Carriers in this study had detailed genomic characterization of normal tissues. Clinical phenotype data beyond cancer is not the focus of the publication, but may be available through the authors.

## Analysis Framework

### What to Look For

For each cohort with POLE truncating variant carriers, assess:

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **Congenital anomalies** | The patient's duplicated IVC argues for developmental POLE effects (Model 4). Any congenital finding in another truncation carrier would be significant |
| **Connective tissue features** | hEDS co-occurs in this patient. If ECM integrity is affected by POLE-driven mutagenesis of collagen/matrix genes, other carriers might show features |
| **Dysautonomia / POTS** | Autonomic dysfunction in multiple carriers would suggest either causal link or shared genetic background |
| **Autoimmune conditions** | ANA/AMA in this patient. Autoimmunity in other carriers would support the mitochondrial stress hypothesis |
| **Endometriosis** | Never investigated in POLE carriers. Even a single additional case would be noteworthy |
| **Non-neoplastic proliferative findings** | PASH, FNH ; has anyone else noted these in POLE carriers? |
| **Multi-system phenotype breadth** | Does any other carrier show findings across >2 phenotype categories? |

### How to Query

1. **Literature search:** PubMed queries for POLE truncating variants with clinical descriptions
   - `POLE frameshift AND (phenotype OR clinical OR congenital OR anomaly)`
   - `POLE truncating AND (connective tissue OR hypermobility OR Ehlers-Danlos)`
   - `POLE carrier AND (autoimmune OR endometriosis OR dysautonomia)`

2. **Direct researcher contact:** The Valle lab (IDIBELL, Barcelona) maintains the largest curated POLE/POLD1 cohort. A direct inquiry about non-neoplastic phenotypes in their 6 LoF carriers would be the single highest-yield action.

3. **PolED database query:** Search for all truncating variants; extract any clinical annotations beyond cancer type.

4. **DECIPHER / Matchmaker Exchange:** Search for POLE truncating variants with multi-system phenotypes.

5. **gnomAD / UK Biobank phenotype data:** For any carriers of rare POLE LoF variants, cross-reference with linked phenotype data (ICD codes for connective tissue disorders, autoimmune conditions, congenital anomalies).

## Expected Findings and Interpretation

### Scenario A: No Non-Neoplastic Findings in Other Carriers

**Interpretation:** Does not exclude a causal link. The ascertainment bias is systematic and severe ; POLE carriers are identified through cancer genetics clinics, and no PPAP phenotyping protocol includes assessment of connective tissue, dysautonomia, congenital anomalies, or autoimmune markers. The absence is expected under current clinical practice.

**Action:** This patient's phenotype argues for **prospective, systematic multi-system phenotyping** of POLE/POLD1 carriers as a research initiative. Propose this to the Valle lab or Palles group as a collaborative study.

### Scenario B: Isolated Reports of Non-Neoplastic Findings

**Interpretation:** Even a single additional truncation carrier with a congenital anomaly, autoimmune condition, or connective tissue feature would be significant, given the small total number of characterized truncation carriers (~6 from Valle 2020 + scattered case reports).

**Action:** Document the finding; assess whether it reaches the affected tissues predicted by Model 4 (haploinsufficiency in high-turnover tissues).

### Scenario C: Pattern of Non-Neoplastic Findings Across Carriers

**Interpretation:** Would constitute strong evidence that POLE truncation variants produce a broader phenotype than exonuclease domain missense variants. Would argue for expanding PPAP phenotyping protocols to include non-neoplastic assessment.

**Action:** Formal publication. Propose revised PPAP clinical guidelines including non-neoplastic phenotyping.

## The Shinbrot Comparator: POLE c.4191_4192delCT

Shinbrot et al. (2019) described a PPAP patient with **POLE c.4191_4192delCT** ; another frameshift variant, though in the C-terminal region (much later in the protein than c.138del). This patient developed an MSI-positive CRC through aberrant DNA methylation (epigenetic MLH1 silencing).

**Key question:** Was any non-neoplastic phenotyping documented for this patient? The c.4191_4192delCT variant truncates POLE in the C-terminal regulatory region ; it preserves exonuclease and polymerase domains but loses regulatory/interaction regions. If this carrier has a narrower phenotype than the c.138del carrier, it would support a genotype-phenotype correlation within truncating variants.

---

## The NTHL1 Double-Carrier: Garcia-Mulero et al. (2019)

Garcia-Mulero et al. (2019) reported a POLE frameshift carrier who was also a carrier of NTHL1 variants. This is relevant because:
- Double-carrier status complicates phenotype attribution
- The POLE frameshift variant in this case should be compared to c.138del
- Any clinical description beyond polyposis would be informative

## Proposed Collaborative Research Initiative

Based on this cross-reference analysis, the following collaborative study is proposed:

**Title:** "Systematic Multi-System Phenotyping of POLE/POLD1 Truncation Variant Carriers: Beyond Polyposis"

**Design:** Retrospective phenotype review + prospective assessment of available carriers

**Collaborators to approach:**
1. **Laura Valle lab** (IDIBELL, Barcelona) ; largest POLE/POLD1 cohort; 6 LoF carriers
2. **Claire Palles group** (Birmingham) ; original PPAP clinical characterization
3. **Robinson/Stratton group** (Sanger Institute) ; NanoSeq carriers with detailed molecular data
4. **PolED database curators** ; access to all reported carriers with functional data

**Assessments:**
- Standard: Cancer type, age, polyp count, TMB, signatures (already collected)
- **Novel:** Beighton score, tilt-table test, connective tissue assessment, autoimmune panel (ANA, AMA, ENA), imaging review for congenital anomalies, endometriosis screening, GI motility assessment

**Outcome:** Determine whether the expanded phenotype observed in the c.138del carrier is unique or represents a previously unrecognized feature of POLE truncation variants.
