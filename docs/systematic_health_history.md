# Systematic Health History Questionnaire

## Purpose

This questionnaire is designed to capture a **whole-body systematic health history** for a patient with POLE c.138del (p.Leu46Phefs*8). The multi-system phenotype — spanning neoplastic, proliferative/stromal, endometrial, congenital developmental, autoimmune/immune, and connective tissue/dysautonomia domains — requires comprehensive phenotyping to understand how POLE dysfunction interacts with other biological systems.

Each section explains **why** the information is relevant to the POLE research framework.

---

## 1. Connective Tissue and Hypermobility

**Why it matters:** hEDS affects extracellular matrix (ECM) and collagen architecture. Altered ECM may modify crypt stem cell niche geometry, tissue-level replication dynamics, and stromal proliferative responses — potentially amplifying or modifying POLE haploinsufficiency effects.

- [ ] Formal Beighton score (9-point scale)?
- [ ] Joint hypermobility (which joints, frequency of subluxations/dislocations)?
- [ ] Skin elasticity, fragility, or translucency?
- [ ] Easy bruising (location, severity, triggers)?
- [ ] Abnormal scarring (keloid, widened, atrophic, cigarette paper)?
- [ ] Wound healing (delayed, poor, normal)?
- [ ] High arched palate or dental crowding?
- [ ] Blue sclerae?
- [ ] Pelvic organ prolapse or uterine prolapse?
- [ ] Hernias (inguinal, umbilical, hiatal)?
- [ ] Family history of hypermobility or EDS?
- [ ] Formal hEDS evaluation date and diagnostic criteria met?

## 2. Dysautonomia and POTS

**Why it matters:** Autonomic dysfunction directly modulates immune function via the vagus nerve (neuroimmune axis). POTS-related immune baseline alterations create uncertainty about ICI response prediction. Autonomic side effects of ATR/CHK1 inhibitors may be poorly tolerated.

- [ ] POTS diagnosis (date, diagnostic method — tilt table, standing test)?
- [ ] Orthostatic intolerance severity (minutes to symptoms, heart rate changes)?
- [ ] Syncope or pre-syncope episodes (frequency, triggers)?
- [ ] Blood pressure patterns (supine vs. standing)?
- [ ] Temperature dysregulation (heat/cold intolerance)?
- [ ] Sweating abnormalities (excessive, reduced, asymmetric)?
- [ ] Bladder dysfunction (frequency, urgency, retention)?
- [ ] Pupil abnormalities?
- [ ] Current POTS management (medications, compression, salt/fluid intake)?
- [ ] Autonomic function testing history (heart rate variability, sudomotor)?

## 3. Gastrointestinal (Beyond Polyposis)

**Why it matters:** GI symptoms have three potential sources in this patient: (1) POLE-driven polyposis/gastritis, (2) endometriosis-related bowel adhesions, and (3) dysautonomia/gastroparesis. Distinguishing these is critical for treatment planning, irAE detection, and drug delivery strategy.

- [ ] Gastroparesis diagnosis (date, gastric emptying study results)?
- [ ] Esophageal dysmotility or dysphagia?
- [ ] Gastroesophageal reflux (severity, management)?
- [ ] SIBO episodes (number, treatment, recurrence pattern)?
- [ ] Baseline nausea severity (daily, episodic, triggers)?
- [ ] Bowel habits (constipation, diarrhea, alternating, transit time)?
- [ ] Abdominal pain pattern (location, timing, relation to meals)?
- [ ] Food intolerances or dietary restrictions?
- [ ] Nutritional status (weight changes, deficiencies, supplements)?
- [ ] Oral medication tolerance and perceived efficacy?
- [ ] History of bowel obstruction or pseudo-obstruction?
- [ ] Enteral or parenteral nutrition history?

## 4. Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)

**Why it matters:** MCAS co-occurs frequently with hEDS/POTS. Chronic mast cell-derived mediators create a pro-tumorigenic microenvironment (angiogenesis, immune suppression, altered epithelial turnover) that may amplify POLE-driven cancer predisposition. Also affects drug tolerability.

- [ ] Flushing episodes (triggers, frequency, duration)?
- [ ] Urticaria (spontaneous, dermographic, cholinergic)?
- [ ] Anaphylaxis or anaphylactoid reactions (frequency, triggers)?
- [ ] Multiple medication sensitivities or "allergies"?
- [ ] Reactions to anesthesia or contrast dye?
- [ ] Bone pain or osteoporosis/osteopenia?
- [ ] Cognitive symptoms ("brain fog") associated with flares?
- [ ] Prior tryptase levels (baseline and during symptoms)?
- [ ] Prior evaluation for MCAS (diagnostic criteria applied)?
- [ ] Response to H1/H2 antihistamines, mast cell stabilizers, or cromolyn?

## 5. Neurological

- [ ] Small fiber neuropathy symptoms (burning, tingling, numbness, allodynia)?
- [ ] Distribution of neuropathic symptoms (length-dependent, patchy, proximal)?
- [ ] Skin biopsy for IENFD (intraepidermal nerve fiber density)?
- [ ] Headache pattern (migraine, tension, daily, positional)?

- [ ] Tethered cord evaluation?
- [ ] Cognitive complaints (memory, concentration, word-finding)?
- [ ] Seizure history?
- [ ] Cranial nerve symptoms (visual, auditory, vestibular)?

## Integration Priority

The most immediately informative items for the POLE research framework are:

1. **MCAS evaluation** (tryptase, urinary PGD2) — determines whether pro-tumorigenic inflammatory modifier is present
2. **Small fiber neuropathy testing** (IENFD skin biopsy) — quantifies enteric neuropathy contribution
3. **Connective tissue phenotype in existing pathology** — colonic crypt architecture, lamina propria collagen
4. **Complete surgical/pathology history** — identifies banked tissue opportunities
5. **Detailed family pedigree** — cancer patterns, hypermobility, autoimmunity for segregation analysis
6. **Gastroparesis quantification** (gastric emptying study) — directly impacts drug delivery planning
7. **Cardiovascular assessment** — additional congenital anomalies would strengthen Model 4
