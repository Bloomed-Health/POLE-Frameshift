# Experimental Protocols

## Overview

Specifications for wet-lab experimental models required to resolve the mechanistic paradox of POLE c.138del.

## Models

### Patient-Derived Organoids (3–6 months)

- **Source:** Tumor and matched normal tissue from surgical resection or biopsy
- **Applications:** Drug screening (ICI in immune co-culture, ATR/PARP/WRN inhibitors), mutational signature dynamics, LOH timing
- **Protocol:** Established colorectal/endometrial organoid culture protocols (Sato et al., 2011)

### Isogenic CRISPR Cell Lines (6–12 months)

- **Target cell lines:** RPE1-hTERT, HCT116, HAP1
- **Genotypes:** c.138del heterozygous, c.138del hemizygous (wild-type allele deleted), P286R, V411L (comparison panel)
- **Assays:** Fluctuation assays (mutation rate), DNA fiber assays (fork speed/processivity), synthetic lethality screens
- **Controls:** Isogenic wild-type parental lines

### Conditional Knock-In Mouse (18–36 months)

- **Design:** Cre-inducible murine equivalent of c.138del
- **Tissues:** Villin-Cre (intestinal), MMTV-Cre (mammary), CMV-Cre (ubiquitous)
- **Endpoints:** Tumor spectrum, latency, LOH requirement, penetrance
- **Crosses:** MMR-null backgrounds (Mlh1, Msh2) to model secondary MMR loss

## Priority

Patient-derived organoids and isogenic cell lines are the highest priority as they directly inform therapeutic decisions and mechanistic understanding within clinically relevant timescales.
