# Multi-Organ Surveillance Protocol

## Rationale

PPAP confers cancer risk across multiple organ systems. Standard PPAP surveillance protocols are based on classical missense variants; the c.138del frameshift may require expanded surveillance given the novel mechanism and potential for broader tissue involvement.

## Recommended Surveillance

### Gastrointestinal

| Modality | Interval | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Colonoscopy with polypectomy** | Every 1–2 years | Standard PPAP recommendation; chromoendoscopy preferred |
| **Upper endoscopy** | Every 2–3 years | Duodenal adenoma screening (per POLD1-PPAP expansion, Frontiers in Oncology 2025) |
| **Capsule endoscopy** | Consider if small bowel symptoms | Small bowel polyp surveillance |

### Gynecologic

| Modality | Interval | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Transvaginal ultrasound** | Annual | Endometrial thickness monitoring |
| **Endometrial biopsy** | If indicated by ultrasound | Low threshold for biopsy given PPAP endometrial cancer risk |

### Molecular Monitoring

| Modality | Interval | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **ctDNA monitoring** | Every 6–8 weeks during active treatment; every 3–6 months in surveillance | Tumor-informed panel (Signatera, FoundationOne Tracker, or Guardant Reveal) |
| **Immune profiling** | At baseline, during treatment, at progression | CyTOF or spectral flow cytometry for treatment response monitoring |

### Additional Screening

| Modality | Interval | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Brain MRI** | Consider annually | POLE-mutant glioblastomas reported in literature |
| **Dermatologic exam** | Annual | Sebaceous neoplasms (overlap with Muir-Torre spectrum) |

## Chemoprevention

- **Aspirin:** Biological rationale from CAPP2 trial in Lynch syndrome; consider n-of-1 design
- **Dose:** 600mg daily (CAPP2 dose) or lower dose pending tolerability
- **Evidence:** Indirect; no PPAP-specific chemoprevention data

## Genetic Counseling

- Reproductive genetic counseling with PGT-M option
- Cascade family testing (acknowledging penetrance uncertainty for truncating variants)
- Psychosocial support for rare variant carriers
